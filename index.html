Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common garage door questions

How much does it cost to replace a garage door?

+

Replacing a garage door can vary widely based on several factors, including the type of door, materials, size, and installation requirements. Homeowners generally find a range of options that can suit different budgets. Higher-end materials like wood or custom designs may increase costs. It's essential to get multiple quotes from contractors to find the best fit for your specific needs.

How much does it cost to replace springs?

+

The cost of replacing garage door springs typically depends on whether you are using torsion or extension springs. Torsion springs are generally more durable and require professional installation, which can affect the overall expense. Addressing spring issues promptly is important, as worn or broken springs can lead to malfunctioning doors and may pose safety risks if not handled correctly.

How much does it cost to replace the motor?

+

Replacing a garage door motor, or opener, usually varies based on the type of motor (chain drive, belt drive, or screw drive) and any additional features, such as smart technology. Higher-quality models often offer better performance and durability, which can justify a higher initial investment.

What materials are garage doors made of?

+

Garage doors are commonly constructed from various materials, each offering distinct benefits and drawbacks. Steel is favored for its durability and low maintenance, while wood provides a classic aesthetic but requires more upkeep. Aluminum is lightweight and rust-resistant, making it suitable for coastal areas. Fiberglass can mimic wood's appearance without the associated maintenance, and vinyl is known for its resilience against dents and scratches. The choice of material often depends on budget, climate, and desired appearance.

How do I maintain my garage door?

+

Regular maintenance is essential for ensuring the longevity and optimal performance of your garage door. This includes lubricating moving parts like hinges, rollers, and tracks to minimize wear and noise. Inspecting the door for signs of wear, such as cracks or rust, is crucial. Checking the balance by manually lifting the door can help you identify issues. Keeping the tracks clean and ensuring weather stripping is intact will also protect your door from the elements and improve energy efficiency.
